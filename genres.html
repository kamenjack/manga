Manga Genres

1. Shōnen

Shōnen manga is targeted at young boys, typically up to 18 years old. These stories often focus on adventure, action, and heroism. Characters usually grow through challenges, fighting villains, and overcoming obstacles.

  • Common Themes: Friendship, courage, rivalry, competition.
  • Popular Examples: Dragon Ball, Naruto, One Piece.

2. Shōjo

Shōjo manga is aimed at young girls, often featuring romance, emotional growth, and interpersonal relationships. Female protagonists face challenges of love, friendship, and self-discovery.

  • Common Themes: Romance, drama, personal growth, friendship.
  • Popular Examples: Fruits Basket, Tokyo Mew Mew, Pretty Soldier Sailor Moon.

3. Seinen

Seinen manga is aimed at young adult men, usually 18–30 years old. These stories often feature more mature themes, complex plots, and sophisticated characters. Seinen can include action, psychological drama, politics, or science fiction.

  • Common Themes: Violence, politics, philosophy, adult romance.
  • Popular Examples: Berserk, Monster, Ghost in the Shell.

4. Josei

Josei manga is aimed at adult women. It explores romance, career, family, and everyday life with realistic situations. The tone is often more mature and reflective compared to shōjo manga.

  • Common Themes: Romance, work-life, personal growth, daily life.
  • Popular Examples: Nodame Cantabile, Honey and Clover.

5. Gekiga

Gekiga is a style of manga developed for adults, featuring serious and sometimes dark themes. Stories are more realistic, dramatic, and often address social, political, or personal issues.

  • Common Themes: Crime, social issues, existential drama.
  • Popular Examples: Akira, Lady Snowblood, works by Yoshihiro Tatsumi.

6. Other Popular Genres

Besides the main categories, manga includes numerous other genres, often overlapping. Some examples include:

  • Sports Manga: Stories about athletic competitions and teamwork (Haikyuu!!, Kuroko's Basketball).
  • Fantasy / Adventure: Magical worlds, quests, or epic journeys (Fairy Tail, Fullmetal Alchemist).
  • Horror: Scary or supernatural stories (Uzumaki, Tomie).
  • Slice of Life: Everyday life, school, and friendships (Azumanga Daioh, March Comes in Like a Lion).
