Manga Genres

Shōnen

Shonen manga, the most mainstream genre aimed primarily at young male readers, typically follows a male protagonist and his companions as they grow stronger while facing increasingly powerful adversaries. These stories often emphasize themes of friendship and perseverance, and the vast majority focus heavily on action and battle scenes.

"Naruto" tells the story of a young ninja determined to become the strongest leader of his village.

Shōjo

In contrast to shonen manga, shoujo manga is primarily aimed at young female readers. Shoujo manga typically features female protagonists and tells stories focused on love, relationships, and everyday life.

"Fruits Basket" follows a girl who discovers a family cursed to turn into zodiac animals, learning about love and acceptance.

Seinen

Seinen manga primarily targets adult readers. Its stories are generally more realistic, often focusing on themes such as work life, suspense, and politics. These works tend to explore deeper issues related to society and human nature.

"Parasyte" tells the story of a high school student who is partially infected by an alien parasite and must fight against other parasitic creatures.

Other Popular Genres

Besides the main categories, manga includes numerous other genres, often overlapping. Some examples include:

  • Sports Manga: Stories about athletic competitions and teamwork.
  • Fantasy / Adventure: Magical worlds, quests, or epic journeys.
  • Horror: Scary or supernatural stories.
  • Slice of Life: Everyday life, school, and friendships.
