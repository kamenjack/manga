Modern Japanese manga emerged after World War II and has flourished for several decades. They form one of the cornerstones of Japan’s anime industry, as many anime productions are adapted from original manga works. While manga literally means “comic book” in Japanese, it is used specifically to refer to Japanese comics because they differ greatly in style, storytelling, and cultural expression from their Western counterparts.

After decades of development, from weekly publications to online serialization, Japanese manga has grown into a wide variety of genres, each with its own distinct style.
